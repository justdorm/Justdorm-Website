NYC Pride Open House Lighting Installation

A lighting installation that covers the NYC AIDS Memorial in pixel-mapped animation, turning the whole structure into one cohesive, art-directable light show

Role
Lighting designer & programmer
Tools
TouchDesigner · DMX · MIDI
Venue
NYC AIDS Memorial — Pride Open House

The memorial as a canvas

I was the lighting designer and programmer for the NYC AIDS Memorial Pride Open House. Fifteen DMX-controlled lights hit every beam of the Memorial, all mapped and controlled with pixel animations in TouchDesigner so the whole memorial lit up as one piece.

Treating the whole memorial as one canvas allowed me to run different Pride-inspired animations across it. These were all created using the Texture Operator family within TouchDesigner.

How it works

Every light was pixel-mapped to its real position around the Memorial. By assigning each fixture an angle and sampling a source animation from 0° (left) to 360° (right), TouchDesigner could drive all fifteen lights from one continuous image — so a single animation sweeps around the entire structure instead of being programmed light by light. Each fixture's sampled pixel was converted into the DMX channel data the lights could read directly.

Working from presets kept the show flexible. I could swap, blend, and retime animations live while keeping every beam in sync.
