Census

An interactive projection that builds itself from its audience, one viewer at a time

Role
Artist & Creative technologist
Tools
TouchDesigner · Depth camera
Venue
SVA Flatiron Gallery — Technological Perseverance
Year
2025

A portrait made of presence

Census is an artwork that builds itself through its audience. At its center is a slowly rotating pie chart, which begins as a blank canvas and gradually fills with unique textures — one for each viewer. Over the course of the exhibition, this accumulation transforms the work into both an archive and a portrait of the event itself.

By its conclusion, Census captures the data of presence — recording the community through the simple act of participation.

How it works

Census premiered at the SVA Flatiron Gallery for the Fall 2025 show "Technological Perseverance." It was developed in TouchDesigner, using a depth camera to detect when a viewer is standing in front of the piece — a glow indicates that their presence is being recorded.

Behind the scenes, a data table stores how long each viewer is present and the angle their slice should originate from, while replicators reference this data to generate the pie chart slices themselves. A random texture from a texture library is chosen for each new viewer, blended with previous slices in case of overlap.
