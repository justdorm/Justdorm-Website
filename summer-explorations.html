Summer Explorations

A gesture-controlled art gallery navigated by hand tracking

Role
Creative technologist
Collaborator
Adam Meyers — imagery & video
Tools
TouchDesigner · Depth Camera
Venue
BitBasel — Miami Art Week 2025

A gallery you conduct

Summer Explorations is a gesture-controlled art gallery: a collection of around 100 AI-generated images and five video overlays, created by my collaborator Adam Meyers, navigated with nothing but hand position tracking.

The project was developed in TouchDesigner and shown during BitBasel Miami Art Week 2025, where visitors stepped up and conducted the gallery themselves.

How it works

The positions of the user's wrists and elbows were tracked using an Orbbec Femto Bolt depth camera. The vertical movement of the left hand controlled the scrolling of the gallery, while the right hand drove the intensity of the video overlays. By calculating the distance between the wrist and elbow for these interactions, people of any height could interact without having to recalibrate the sensor height. Two on-screen rectangles indicated the position of each hand, and they shifted color with horizontal hand movement, giving visitors a third way to play with the visuals.
